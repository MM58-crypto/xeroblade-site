How can one prove the non-existence of something?
To prove the existence of material things, we must perceive them through one or more of our senses — sight, sound, taste, and so on. However, we cannot be certain that something does not exist based solely on the amount of information available to us today. Gases, for example, are mostly colourless and odourless, yet they undeniably exist.
In other words, the inability to perceive something does not necessarily mean that it is nonexistent.

It is, of course, impossible to quantify everything in the universe. The only logical answer I can think of to definitively determine the non-existence of something is to know everything in the entire universe — to be all-knowing. This, however, is impossible for a human being. Only God possesses this ability.

Even if someone hypothetically managed to know everything, they could only do so up to a certain point in time. They would have no way of keeping pace with what comes after — new matter forming, new creatures being born, the universe continuing to unfold.
